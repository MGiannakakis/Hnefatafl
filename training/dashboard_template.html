How to read this dashboard

Cadence. The training callback rewrites the data after every PPO update (every plot_freq timesteps, default 2048). The page polls it every 5 seconds — leave it open. All x-axes are environment timesteps (the learning agent's moves only).

Self-play distorts naïve reading. Two things lie to you here: (1) episode reward is inflated by shaping — the engine grants small per-move rewards (captures, progress), so the agent can post positive reward while losing every game; (2) the opponent is a moving target — every opponent_update_freq steps the current policy is snapshotted in as the new opponent, so reward and losses can legitimately dip right after each swap while the agent is actually improving. Judge trends between swaps, not absolute levels.

Health at a glance. Explained variance climbing toward 1 and approx-KL / clip-fraction in their healthy bands (≈0.01–0.05 and ≈0.1–0.3) mean stable learning. Then look at the boards below to see what is being learned — curves can look perfect while every game ends the same degenerate way.

How to read the boards

Final position of the most recent completed training episodes, newest first. Dark discs are attackers, light discs are defenders, the gold K is the king; the shaded centre square is the throne. The caption gives the winner and the engine's termination reason.

Termination reasons are your win-rate proxy: training ATK, progress looks like "King escaped" captions giving way to "King captured". The king's final square shows which escape lanes are left open; thinning defender counts with "King escaped" means the attacker trades well but never blockades — a shaping-reward exploit worth catching. Draw pile-ups ("moves limit", "threefold repetition") late in training mean the self-play equilibrium is degenerating into mutual avoidance, not success.
